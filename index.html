Reconfigurable Fourier Filtering Imaging Bilayer Metasurface

Ping Sun1, Michele Cotrufo2

1The Institute of Optics, University of Rochester, Rochester, NY, USA
2The Institute of Optics, University of Rochester, Rochester, NY, USA
Dated: December 01, 2024

Abstract

This paper demonstrates one bilayer Silicon metasurface with rhombus-arranged air-holes, enabling a switch between 0.1-0.5 NA 360° Azimuthal 3D edge detection with high polarization-insensitivity and isotropy and 1D center blur & denoising by air gap tuning.

Fourier signal filtering remains a continuously prominent topic across various disciplines, including signal processing, imaging, and computational signal analysis. Leveraging the versatile reconfigurability of flat photonics, this paper introduces a bilayer metasurface structure capable of achieving multiple filtering functionalities at a single operating wavelength. Specifically, through tuning the air gap between two layers, the device can achieve switching from 360° Azimuthal 3D edge detection with high polarization-insensitivity and isotropy to 1D center blur & denoising for noisy image input.
